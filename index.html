Saroop Singh's Legacy

Saroop Singh dominated Malayan athletics from 1937 to 1957, setting multiple records in the half-mile and 880-yard events. His exceptional speed and endurance made him a household name across Southeast Asia. This archive preserves his remarkable achievements through original newspaper clippings and family photographs.

Featured Articles

Half-mile Record Broken at Selangor AAA Meet

24 July 1937 Morning Tribune Kuala Lumpur

Saroop Singh broke the half-mile record at the Selangor Amateur Athletic Association meeting, knocking off a second from the existing record of 2 mins. 6 4/5 secs.

First Malayan Sikh Ladies Athletic Meet

17 April 1949 Indian Daily Mail Kuala Lumpur

Historic first athletic meet for Malayan Sikh ladies, with Mrs. J. A. Thivy performing the prize distribution ceremony. Saroop Singh contributed a cup to the event.

Sikh Runner's State Record For Half-Mile

15 July 1957 The Straits Times Kuala Lumpur

Saroop Singh set a State record for the half-mile at the Selangor Athletic Association championships, showcasing continued excellence in distance running.

The Saroop Singh Digital Archive

This archive preserves the athletic legacy of Saroop Singh, one of Malaya's most celebrated athletes who dominated track and field events from 1937-1957. His achievements in the half-mile and 880-yard races set standards that inspired generations of athletes. Each newspaper clipping has been carefully digitized to share his remarkable story with researchers, historians, and sports enthusiasts worldwide.
